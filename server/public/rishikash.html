What to Know About Rishikesh

Set against the backdrop of the Himalayas and with the pristine Ganga flowing through it, Rishikesh is one of the major tourist and pilgrimage hubs in northern India. Rishikesh is commonly referred to as the ‘yoga capital of the world’. Visitors come here from all over the world to learn yoga and meditation.

It has numerous ashrams, some internationally known for philosophical studies, yoga, and wellness. The International Yoga Festival (IYF) draws thousands every year. Rishikesh is also mentioned in Skanda Purana and Ramayana – where Lord Rama did penance after killing Ravana.

In 1968, The Beatles visited Rishikesh to learn transcendental meditation. They composed many songs here, some featured in the White Album. John Lennon even recorded a song called 'The Happy Rishikesh Song'.
