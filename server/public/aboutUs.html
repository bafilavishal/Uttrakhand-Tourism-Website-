About Uttarakhand

Nestled in the lap of the Himalayas, Uttarakhand is a land of breathtaking landscapes, serene rivers, and spiritual heritage. Known as the "Devbhumi" (Land of the Gods), this northern Indian state is home to some of the most revered pilgrimage sites, including Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri, collectively known as the Char Dham.

Adventure enthusiasts can indulge in trekking, river rafting, camping, and paragliding in destinations like Rishikesh, Chopta, and Nainital. The state also boasts tranquil hill stations such as Mussoorie, Almora, and Kausani, offering spectacular views of the snow-capped Himalayas.
